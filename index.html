What is Climate Change?

Climate change is a change in global or regional climate patterns. It is attributed to the greenhouse effect, which is warming that results when the atmosphere traps heat radiating from Earth toward space. Some gases that contribute to the greenhouse effect are Nitrous Oxide, Water Vapor, Carbon Dioxide, and Methane. Climate change changes Earth's geological, biological, and ecological systems. For example, some effects of climate change are:

  • Rising Sea Levels
  • More Wildfires
  • Increased Extreme Weather
  • Increased Health Risks
  • Destruction of Animal Habitats

Human Involvment in Climate Change

Due to the things humans do in their daily lives, humans are contributing to climate change. According to climate.nasa.gov, there is a more than 95 percent probability that human activities over the past 50 years have warmed our planet. This includes things such as the burning of fossil fuels, which released carbon dioxide gas into the atmosphere, pollution, food waste, electricity use, and transportation.
